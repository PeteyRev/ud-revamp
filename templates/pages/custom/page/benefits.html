How much do you sit?

Modern office life is full of conveniences that make our workday easier. Email, chat, remote meetings, Skype - all this and more have made it simpler than ever to communicate with one another and get the job done. But there’s a huge drawback: we’re sitting too much. In recent years, scientists have studied the effects of what they’re calling “prolonged sitting” or “sitting disease,” and the news isn’t good. Prolonged sitting is clearly and indisputably linked to diabetes, heart disease, cancer, premature death, musculoskeletal disorders, disrupted metabolic functions, obesity, and depression. What’s more, the health impact of prolonged sitting is the same regardless of other factors such as whether you smoke, exercise, or are overweight. The bottom line (no pun intended) is that prolonged sitting is bad for everyone.

Benefits of standing

Fortunately, you can stop the cycle of sitting simply by changing your workstation. The health benefits of using an adjustable height desk are numerous:

  • An average 54% reduction in musculoskeletal pain
  • An average productivity boost of 42%
  • Lessening of fatigue, confusion, and tension
  • Improved energy, focus, productivity, collaboration, and creativity
  • Reduced risk of obesity, heart disease, diabetes, cancer, and depression
  • Increased calorie burn while standing - 50 calories per hour

Did you know?

Employers, did you know that back pain is the leading cause of work-related disability in people under 45, and is responsible for $1 billion spent per year in workers’ comp and medical expenses? Back pain also results in 93 million lost work days each year. Clearly, a sit-stand desk is beneficial not only for the workers who use them, but for a company’s bottom line. Two-thirds of office workers wish their companies offered sit-stand workstations, and 82% of workers believe they would be more productive using a sit-stand desk. This is a case where everyone wins. Employees are happier and healthier, and companies save money on medical costs and missed work days, as well as enjoy a boost from more productive workers. Join the sit-stand revolution to see how much better you feel and how much more you can get done!
